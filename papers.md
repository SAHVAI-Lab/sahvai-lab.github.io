## AI & Deep Learning Applications

### Attention-Enhanced Deep Learning for Hemorrhagic vs Ischemic Stroke Classification (2026) ⭐
**Che S, Che S, Liu X, Du P, Wang Z, Chen Y, Hu Y, Kong L**  
*Eur J Med Res.* 2026 Mar;10.1186/s40001-025-03784-w

**GCDCNet Model** - A lightweight attention-enhanced deep learning model for automated classification of hemorrhagic vs. ischemic stroke on CT imaging. Achieved 99.26% accuracy with only 2.7M parameters using SCSE attention mechanism, dilated convolution, and channel shuffling for feature optimization.

**Key Performance Metrics:**
- Accuracy: 99.26%
- Recall: 99.33%
- F1 Score: 99.13%
- Precision: 98.94%
- AUC-ROC: 0.9996
- Parameters: 2.7M (lightweight)

**Dataset:** 964 hemorrhagic strokes + 1551 ischemic strokes (public dataset)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Early stroke screening
- Emergency triage
- Telemedicine support
- Optimizing medical resource allocation

**Technical Highlights:**
- Based on GhostNet lightweight architecture
- SCSE (Spatial & Channel Squeeze-Excitation) attention mechanism
- Grad-CAM visualization shows high consistency with clinician focus areas
- Outperformed ConvNeXt, EfficientNet, HRNet, and ViT models

**Relevance to SAHVAI:** Attention mechanisms and lightweight architecture could inform improvements to SAHVAI-3D/4D classification models. The Grad-CAM interpretability approach aligns with clinical validation needs.

[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41776569/) | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40001-025-03784-w)
